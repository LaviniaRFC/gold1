In my country families are much bigger than
in many other places. In my house alone we have two aunts, three uncles, my grandparents and, of course, my mother, father, brothers and sisters. We are very happy living together, even if sometimes our values are different from generation to generation.

In my country children usually leave home when they go to college. Leaving our parents’ house makes us far more independent and autonomous. Many of us get jobs and never go back to our original homes. I think that’s one of the reasons we have so many highways: we travel a lot...

In some places in my country, elder parents normally live with their daughters, even if
they have many sons. This tradition is not
so common in bigger cities as it is in small towns, but even nowadays some girls don’t get married because they will have to take care of their parents in the future.
4
In the country I come from there isn’t so much pressure on young people to go to college immediately after they finish high school. Many leave their parents’ home, get jobs, and only then start higher education. I believe it is much better this way, as it allows us to make more mature career choices.
5
In my country the elderly are seen as wise and children learn from a very early age that their older relatives, even the ones who have already died, must be respected and honored. Oh, and meals are special moments shared by parents and their children. I’m pretty sure this helps our families be far more harmonious than in many other places.
