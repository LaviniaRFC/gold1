And yet, city life isn’t easy. While
the city may be a fertile ground for intellectual production, it is also a deeply unnatural and overwhelming environment. Scientists have examined how the city affects the brain, and the results are alarming. After spending a few minutes on a crowded city street, the brain is less able to hold things in memory, and suffers from reduced self- control. While it’s long been recognized that city life is exhausting, this new research suggests that cities actually make people dumber, sometimes dramatically so.

Natural settings, in contrast, don’t require the same amount of cognitive effort. They are full of objects that automatically capture our attention, yet without triggering a negative emotional response. The part of your brain that directs attention can relax deeply, recovering itself. Nature is surprisingly beneficial for the brain. Studies have demonstrated, for instance, that hospital patients recover more quickly when they can see trees from their windows. Even glimpses of nature improve brain performance because they provide a mental break from the urban scenario. This also helps explain why, according to several studies, children with attention-deficit disorder have fewer symptoms in natural settings. When surrounded by trees and animals, they are less likely to have behavioral problems and are better able to focus on a particular task.
