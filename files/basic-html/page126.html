Is that why some people end up moving back to where they came from?
This happens because people’s needs change over time. Both the city and the country have several pros and cons. What matters is what is more important or necessary to you.
Can you elaborate on that, Professor?
OK, let’s say two people moved to the city to find better jobs and more leisure activities. They get used to the hectic pace of life and they love the cultural variety and endless possibilities. Time goes by. They reach a more stable financial situation, get married, have kids, and realize they want to slow down and find a quieter, safer place to raise the kids. As a result, they move back to the countryside.
I see your point now. As a matter of fact, I guess that’s why my parents moved back to the country when I was born.
And since you wanted a more exciting life, you moved back to the city, right?
Yes, I suppose so. I left for college and never really looked back, but now I’m wondering if I’ll return one day.
It’s not a rule or anything. Our priorities are constantly changing and we often make adjustments in our lives because of that. I, for instance, raised my kids here in the city and it was fine, but my oldest son has been thinking of moving to the country.

I was actually amazed at the ritualistic way in which they conducted every step of the meal. It was about a lot more than just eating. The only part that really killed me was using chopsticks.

Professor, nowadays lots of people move out of big metropolitan areas due to the stress of what they call “big city life”. Do you think that’s the best solution?
Not necessarily, small towns have problems of their own. There are considerably less employment opportunities, and not much room to grow. That’s why thousands of people leave small towns and arrive in big cities every day searching for a better life.
So, where is the best quality of life then?
I believe our quality of life is not determined by whether we live in a big city or small town, but rather by whether the place we live in suits us or not. Enjoying where you live is a key factor for your well-being. That explains why some people will find better quality of life in a big city, for instance.
